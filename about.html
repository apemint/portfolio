About Me

Hello! As you can probably tell from the nav bar, my name is Andres Alfonso Escobar, I usually go by Andy though. I am an aspiring developer based in the East coast, tri-state area.

I graduated from Rutgers University in 2019, with a Bachelors in Russian Language and Literature, and a Minor in Economics. Despite this, I am creating a carreer in Web Development. I have always had a huge interest in computers, and with the recent COVID19 situation, I had the extra free time to further explore my interests in computers throughfree online resources like freecodecamp.org and the theodinproject.com among others. This spurred my interest, and I decided to take the plunge in joining a bootcamp for its more structured approach to learning, along with being able to collaborate with other starting like-minded developers.

Besides learning web development, my interest vary greatly. In college, I practiced Kendo, and was nearly the equivalent of a black belt before I had to take a hiatus due to injuries and time constraints. I also love learning languages and learning about foreign cultures. I grew up speaking some Spanish, can speak intermediate level Russian, and studied some Japanese. Besides via language, I love exploring cultures through TV shows, music, and mostly through food, and traveling, though I rarely get the opportunity to do so.
